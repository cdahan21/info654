Day and Nightlife in NYC

By Rachel S. Khmelnitsky

Here is NYC sometimes you try to think of stuff to fill up time between meals and museums. So what to do inbetween in NYC? Here I have provided lists of prompts divided by day, night, and by season for some ideas for your visit!

For the native and new New Yorkers, some ideas on what to do on your day off!

Key: For activities that are especially kid-friendly, I have included 🧃, for 21+ activities I have included 🍷

NYC Daylife

  • Walk around the parks
  • Tour some of the city’s architecture
  • Do a statue tour
  • Walk over one, or all, of our bridges
  • Find a pop up (I suggest not in the Winter if you see a line)
  • Grab afternoon tea 🧃
  • Grab coffee or kava
  • Window shop, or just shop, at a flea or farmer’s market
  • Eat and buy trinkets at a seasonal food festival
  • Take the Staten Island ferry 🧃
  • Visit Governor's Island
  • Visit a historic church
  • Visit a historic house (Revolutionary war history is strong here) 🧃
  • Hit up a botanical garden or zoo 🧃
  • Visit a library! 🧃
  • Hit up an arcade 🧃
  • Relax at a banya/bathhouse 🧃
  • Hit up a christmas market
  • See iconic NYC winter locations: Rockefeller, Radio City, the 34th street Macy's
  • Ice skate 🧃
  • See Santa 🧃
  • Get some free tulips at
  • Find the cherry blossoms blooming 🧃
  • Picnic at a park 🧃
  • Bike around the city (without frost bite)
  • Hit up the amusement park in coney island 🧃
  • Chill by the ocean in Far Rockaway 🧃
  • Catch a baseball game – big or small leagues 🧃
  • Watch some basketball or handball tournaments in local parks
  • It’s apple cider season in the big apple! (best farmer’s market season in my opinion)
  • Go through a corn maze (Yes, we have farms in the city too, also some community gardens do it too) 🧃
  • Find a pumpkin smash event after Halloween 🧃
  • Dog parades, where the dog’s dress up! 🧃

Prospect Park

1904 Surf Ave
Brooklyn, NY 11224
United States

Nearest Train Stop: "7th Avenue, Prospect Park, Park Side AVE, 15th St and Prospect Park, and Eastern Pkwy-Brooklyn Museum"

Train Available to Stop: Q, B, F, G 2, 3, 4-train

Prospect Park is Brooklyn's version of Central Park. You can bike or skate on the roads, find or play baseball in the feilds, have a picnic in the day or watch stars at night. The park hosts event, has many walking trails, bridges, and plazas to walk through, and some wate spaces to watch ducks and swans. Nearby the North exit is the main Brooklyn Public Library, The Prospect Park Zoo, the Brooklyn Museum, the Brooklyn Botanical Gardens and Grand Army Plaza.

Crotona Park

Chorlotte St
Bronx , NY 10457
United States

Nearest Train Stop: "174 St"

Train Available to Stop: 2 and 5 train

Crotona park features a nature walk, nature center, a lovely lake with wildlife and fishing spots, man-made structures, play grounds, and sport courts, including 20 tennis courts. Within the park is also the Crotona play center, hosting an indoor bathhouse and public pool.

Flushing Meadows Corona Park

A historic park for being the location of two 20th century world fairs, the big globe, the park also features numerous activities and events. From tennis courts, picnic spots, and an indoor recreation center, and nearby water activities in the warm season.

Favorite Spots

NYC tends to have the reputation of not being so kid friendly at night as kids do have a bed-time even in the city that never sleeps. So, below here I have some recommendations for one of my favorite evening activites which kids can participate in, dine-in movie theaters! Dine-in have grown in popularity here in the city. Some theaters even do special showings of older movies. It's a nice step up from a classic movie experience and more casual then a normal dinner out. So adult, kids, and teens can have an even night out experience.

My second card here however, is adult only. I feature 3 of my favorite spots for an evening out. Enjoy!

Brooklyn, NY 11249
United States

40 Bogart St
Brooklyn, NY 11206
United States

Syndicated is both a stand alone bar, and a dine-in theater. In their small theater with long booth style seats, you can order food and drinks to the table.

Adult-only spots

×

8th Street Wineceller

28 W 8th St
New York, NY 10011
United States

Nearest Train Stop: "West 4"

Train Available to Stop: A, B, C, D, E, F, M Train

Just half a block from Washington Square park, 8th Street Wineceller is a small wine bar with a large selection and appetizers. The vibe is very calm and typically quiet. Exceptions is the summer and weekend. Nearby are also some independent shops and Greenwich Villiage's live music and comedy hubs. I recommend coming by for a slow down in the night or for the start to the night.

Wiggle Room

9 Avenue A
New York, NY 10009
United States

Nearest Train Stop: 2 AV"

Train Available to Stop: F Train

Wiggle Room is a Bar upstairs and night club downstair. They have a classy but also 1970s vibe. Situated in Alphabet City in the Lower East Side, near by are number of other venues for live music, retro feels, for clubbing type feel. You might also find some poetry or art events as the neighborhood is home to artist.

The Ten Bells Brooklyn

65 Irving Ave
Brooklyn, NY 11237
United States

Nearest Train Stop: "Morgan AVE"

Train Available to Stop: L-train

The North East Coast is where oysters are sourced, New York being a main locations part of that sourcing. Therefore, I suggest getting some while you're here. September-December is oyster season, but you'll find them anytime of year as well. You'll find a lot of places doing oyster happy hours, but Ten Bells is my personal favorite $1 happy hour. Near by is Maria Hernandez Park, some shops, more dessert, and a few club and music venues.
