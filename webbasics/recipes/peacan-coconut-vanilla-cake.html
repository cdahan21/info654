Pecan Coconut Vanilla Cake

Layered vanilla cake with shredded coconut and chopped pecans, frosted with a creamy cream cheese buttercream.

Decadent, nutty, and perfectly balanced ‚Äî a show-stopping cake for birthdays or special occasions.

Equipment

  • 3 8-inch cake pans
  • Piping tip (optional)

Ingredients

Cake:

  • 2 2/3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 12 tbsp unsalted butter, softened and cut into pieces
  • 1 1/2 cups buttermilk, room temperature
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp almond extract
  • 1 cup sweetened shredded coconut
  • 1 cup coarsely chopped pecans

Frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp almond extract
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 8 cups powdered sugar

Optional Decoration:

  • 1/2 cup pecan halves, finely chopped/whole
  • 1/2 cup toasted coconut, finely chopped

Directions

Make the Cake:

  1. Preheat oven to 350¬∞F. Grease and flour three 8-inch cake pans, lining the bottoms with parchment if desired.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. With a mixer on medium speed, add butter pieces one at a time, mixing until sandy and incorporated.
  4. Whisk buttermilk, eggs, vanilla, and almond extract in a measuring cup, then slowly drizzle into batter while mixing on low. Scrape bowl sides as needed.
  5. Fold in shredded coconut and chopped pecans until evenly combined.
  6. Divide batter evenly among prepared pans. Bake 30 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean or with moist crumbs. Cool 10‚Äì15 minutes in pans, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.

Make the Frosting:

  1. In a large bowl, beat butter and cream cheese until creamy.
  2. Add vanilla, almond extract, and salt. Mix to combine.
  3. Gradually add powdered sugar, mixing until smooth and creamy.

Assemble the Cake:

  1. Level cakes if necessary. Place one layer on serving plate and spread an even layer of frosting. Repeat with remaining layers.
  2. Apply a thin crumb coat around the outside and refrigerate 15 minutes.
  3. Frost the entire cake evenly. Use remaining frosting to pipe decorative swirls if desired.
  4. Optionally, mix chopped pecans and toasted coconut and press along sides or sprinkle on top of swirls.

Notes

  • Ensure butter and cream cheese are at room temperature for smooth frosting.
  • Level cakes for an even layered look and stable frosting.
  • Decorative topping adds texture and visual appeal but is optional.
